Research question

What correlations are there (if any) between having confidence in you country, its government, feeling secure and being open to/trusting of others? What other factors might play a part? How have these changed over time?

Trail #1 WVS

At the WVS website, I go to the data and chose a wave. It's not possible to chose all countries available at once so I have to patiently click all of the countries as I really want to get as much data as possible to see if I can find an answer to my question. I then look at some surveys that correspond to my question. Such as questions about the government, trust in people, satisfaction in life, nationality pride, the press and human rights. Then I look at the countries threw the years (for as many waves the country has data that is) It isn't easy to get an overview at this site. The data is very centered on the country and it's hard to compare different variables. Therefore it's hard to answer my question. I did find some small correlation between confidence in the government and the general trust. Even though I couldn't fully answer my question using this site's visualizations, it was intresting to see the development in the different waves for countries.

Trail #2 my visualization

In my visualization you can brush the different variables to see if perhaps there's a correlation between general trust in people and a perceived democratic government. The brushed data gathers in a list below the parallel coordinates were sortable data is available for further correlations.
